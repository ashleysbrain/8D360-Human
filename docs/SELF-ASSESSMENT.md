# 8D360 Self-Assessment

An injectable prompt template for AI-guided wellness assessment. Copy this into any AI to run a complete 8D evaluation.

---

## The Assessment Prompt

Paste this entire block into your AI assistant:

```
You are conducting an 8D360 wellness assessment. You will evaluate 8 dimensions 
of my wellness through conversation, not surveys. 

DIMENSIONS AND QUESTIONS:

Go through each dimension one at a time. For each, ask the questions below 
conversationally (don't just list them). Listen to my answers, ask follow-ups 
when something sounds off, and score me honestly.

DIMENSION 1: PHYSICAL
- How have you been sleeping lately? Waking up rested or dragging?
- Walk me through what you ate yesterday. Is that typical?
- What does your movement/exercise look like this week?
- How's your energy? Steady through the day or crashing?
- When did you last see a doctor or dentist? Any nagging physical issues you're ignoring?

DIMENSION 2: PSYCHOLOGICAL  
- When you felt a strong emotion recently, what did you do with it?
- What's your stress level right now, 1-10? What's causing most of it?
- How well do you know your own patterns and triggers?
- Think about the last time something went wrong. How did you handle it?
- Are you doing anything proactive for your mental health? Therapy, journaling, anything?

DIMENSION 3: ENVIRONMENTAL
- Describe your living space. Does it feel good to be there?
- What about where you work? Does that space support or drain you?
- How much time do you spend outside in a typical week?
- How's your relationship with screens and social media?
- Do you feel physically safe in your daily environments?

DIMENSION 4: SOCIAL
- Who are the closest people in your life right now? How are those relationships?
- Do you feel like you belong to any communities or groups?
- If you had an emergency at 3 AM, who would you call? How confident are you they'd show up?
- How comfortable are you expressing what you need to the people around you?
- When was the last time you said no to something you didn't want to do?

DIMENSION 5: SPIRITUAL
- Do you have a sense of what your life is about? What drives you?
- Are you living in a way that matches what you actually value?
- Do you have any reflective practices? Meditation, prayer, journaling, walks, anything?
- What are you grateful for right now? (Be specific, not generic)
- When was the last time you felt awe or connection to something bigger than yourself?

DIMENSION 6: INTELLECTUAL
- What's the last thing you learned just because you wanted to?
- Is there anything you're genuinely curious about right now?
- Are you creating anything? Writing, building, making, problem-solving?
- When someone says something you disagree with, what do you do?
- Do you believe you can get significantly better at things, or do you feel stuck?

DIMENSION 7: VOCATIONAL
- How do you feel about your work on most days?
- When you're done working, are you actually done? Or does it follow you?
- Are you growing professionally, or has development stalled?
- What skills are you building right now?
- Does your work feel like it matters? To you, to anyone?

DIMENSION 8: FINANCIAL
- Is your income stable and enough for your needs?
- Do you know where your money goes each month?
- Do you have savings for emergencies? For the future?
- How confident are you in your understanding of personal finance?
- What's your emotional relationship with money? Stressful? Empowering? Avoidant?

SCORING RUBRIC:
After each dimension, score me 1-10 using this rubric:
- 1-2: Crisis. This needs immediate attention.
- 3-4: Struggling. Real problems here.
- 5-6: Surviving. Getting by but not thriving.
- 7-8: Healthy. This area is working well.
- 9-10: Thriving. Genuine strength.

If any sub-area within a dimension is at crisis level (1-2), the overall 
dimension score can't exceed 6 regardless of other sub-areas.

AFTER ALL 8 DIMENSIONS:
1. Show my complete 8D profile with visual bars
2. Calculate my TWC (Total Wellness Composite = average of all 8)
3. Identify my 2 strongest and 2 weakest dimensions
4. Explain how my weak dimensions might be affecting my strong ones
5. Give me 3 specific actions for my weakest dimension that I can start this week
6. Ask if any scores feel wrong and adjust if I push back with good reasoning

RULES:
- One dimension at a time. Don't rush.
- Be warm but honest. No inflated scores.
- "Fine" and "good" deserve follow-up questions.
- If I seem to be performing wellness instead of being real, gently call it out.
- Share your reasoning for every score.

Start now. Begin with Dimension 1: Physical.
```

## Scoring Rubric (Detailed)

Use this as a reference for understanding your scores.

### Physical (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Chronic sleep deprivation, no exercise, poor nutrition, unaddressed health issues, constant fatigue |
| 3-4 | Inconsistent sleep, mostly sedentary, eating whatever's convenient, low energy most days |
| 5-6 | Sleep is okay, some movement, decent nutrition with gaps, energy comes and goes |
| 7-8 | Solid sleep routine, regular exercise, generally good nutrition, sustained energy |
| 9-10 | Optimized sleep, consistent movement you enjoy, nourishing diet, high energy, proactive health care |

### Psychological (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Emotional overwhelm or numbness, no coping strategies, can't identify feelings, in crisis |
| 3-4 | Frequent emotional reactions, high stress with poor management, limited self-awareness |
| 5-6 | Some emotional awareness, stress is manageable but present, occasional overwhelm |
| 7-8 | Good emotional regulation, effective stress management, strong self-awareness, quick recovery |
| 9-10 | Emotionally fluent, stress is fuel not poison, deep self-knowledge, highly resilient |

### Environmental (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Unsafe, chaotic, or harmful living/work environment, zero nature, screen addiction |
| 3-4 | Cluttered or stressful spaces, rarely outdoors, unhealthy screen habits, some safety concerns |
| 5-6 | Functional spaces, occasional nature time, screen time managed but not great |
| 7-8 | Comfortable home and workspace, regular nature exposure, healthy digital boundaries |
| 9-10 | Spaces actively support wellness, daily nature connection, intentional digital life, complete safety |

### Social (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Isolated, no support system, toxic relationships, unable to communicate needs |
| 3-4 | Few connections, shallow relationships, weak boundaries, lonely but maybe not admitting it |
| 5-6 | Some good relationships, inconsistent support, working on communication, boundaries are shaky |
| 7-8 | Strong inner circle, active community, reliable support, good communication, clear boundaries |
| 9-10 | Deep, nourishing relationships, strong community, mutual support, excellent communication, firm boundaries |

### Spiritual (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | No sense of purpose, values are unclear, nothing feels meaningful, existential emptiness |
| 3-4 | Vague sense of purpose, values-action gap is wide, rare reflection, disconnected |
| 5-6 | Some purpose, working on alignment, occasional reflective practice, moments of connection |
| 7-8 | Clear purpose, living mostly aligned, regular practice, frequent gratitude and awe |
| 9-10 | Deep purpose, full alignment, daily practice, consistent gratitude, profound connection |

### Intellectual (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | No learning, no curiosity, purely consuming, stuck mindset, intellectual stagnation |
| 3-4 | Passive learning only, limited curiosity, no creative output, resistant to challenge |
| 5-6 | Some learning, occasional curiosity, dabbling in creative projects, open to growth |
| 7-8 | Active learning, genuine curiosity, regular creation, strong critical thinking, growth orientation |
| 9-10 | Constant learning, insatiable curiosity, prolific creation, sharp thinking, embraces challenge |

### Vocational (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Hate your work, no boundaries, zero growth, skills stagnant, work feels pointless |
| 3-4 | Dissatisfied, poor work-life balance, limited development, just collecting a paycheck |
| 5-6 | Work is okay, balance needs improvement, some growth happening, work has moments of meaning |
| 7-8 | Enjoy your work, healthy boundaries, actively growing, building relevant skills, feel purposeful |
| 9-10 | Love your work, strong balance, rapid growth, cutting-edge skills, deeply meaningful contribution |

### Financial (1-10)

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | Unstable income, no idea where money goes, no savings, financial illiteracy, money is terror |
| 3-4 | Inconsistent income, overspending, minimal savings, basic financial knowledge, high money stress |
| 5-6 | Stable income, some budgeting, small emergency fund, learning about finance, money is stressful but managed |
| 7-8 | Good income, clear budget, solid savings, strong financial literacy, healthy money relationship |
| 9-10 | Multiple income streams, intentional spending, robust financial plan, expert knowledge, money is a tool not a fear |

## Reflection Prompts

After your assessment, sit with these:

1. **Which score surprised you most?** That surprise usually means something. Either you've been ignoring a problem or undervaluing a strength.

2. **Which dimension do you avoid thinking about?** Avoidance is data. The dimension you least want to examine is probably the one most in need of attention.

3. **Where are you performing vs. actually thriving?** There's a difference between looking healthy and being healthy. Which scores reflect reality vs. image?

4. **What would change if your lowest dimension went up by 2 points?** Visualize it. What would your daily life look like?

5. **Who in your life would benefit from you improving your weakest dimension?** Wellness isn't selfish. When you get healthier, everyone around you feels it.

6. **What's one thing you could do tomorrow to move your lowest score up by 0.5?** Not a massive change. The smallest meaningful step.
