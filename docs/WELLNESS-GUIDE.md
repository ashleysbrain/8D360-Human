# Your 8D360 Wellness Guide

This is written for you. Not for an AI. Not for a developer. For you, the human reading this who wants to feel better across more of your life.

---

## What Is 8D360?

8D360 is a way to look at your entire life and figure out what's working, what's not, and what to do about it.

It breaks your wellness into 8 dimensions:

1. **Physical** - your body
2. **Psychological** - your mind and emotions
3. **Environmental** - your spaces
4. **Social** - your people
5. **Spiritual** - your purpose
6. **Intellectual** - your growth
7. **Vocational** - your work
8. **Financial** - your money

Each one gets a score from 1 to 10. You can see exactly where you're strong and where you need help. Then you do something about it.

That's it. That's the framework.

---

## Why Bother?

Because you've probably been optimizing one or two dimensions and wondering why you still feel off.

You might be in great shape physically but isolated socially. Financially stable but spiritually empty. Intellectually sharp but emotionally a mess.

The point of 8D360 isn't to be perfect in all 8 areas. It's to make sure no single area is so low that it's dragging everything else down. Balance isn't a luxury. It's the foundation.

## How to Use This Daily

### Morning (2 minutes)

Pick one dimension to pay attention to today. Just one. You're not tracking all 8 every day. That's exhausting.

Monday: Physical. Am I going to move my body today?
Tuesday: Psychological. How am I feeling? What do I need?
Wednesday: Environmental. Is my space supporting me?
Thursday: Social. Am I going to connect with a human today?
Friday: Spiritual. What matters to me right now?
Saturday: Intellectual. What am I curious about?
Sunday: Full check-in. Quick 1-10 for all 8 dimensions.

You can rotate however you want. The point is daily awareness without daily overwhelm.

### During the Day

When something feels off, ask yourself: "Which dimension is this?" You'd be surprised how often the answer isn't what you'd expect.

Feeling anxious at work? Maybe it's not Psychological. Maybe your Financial dimension is tanking and it's bleeding into your work stress.

Can't focus on anything? Maybe it's not Intellectual. Maybe your Physical dimension is shot because you slept four hours. Research (Pinggal et al., 2026) shows that attention lapses and "zoning out" are often vigilance regulation issues tied to sleep-wake physiology, not willpower failures. And sleep debt has molecular consequences that don't disappear with one good night (Cheng et al., 2026).

The framework gives you a map. Use it to stop treating symptoms and start finding root causes.

### Evening (2 minutes)

One question: "Did my focus dimension get any attention today?"

If yes, great. If no, why not? No judgment. Just noticing.

## How to Track Progress

### Weekly Check-In

Every Sunday (or whatever day works for you), do a quick score of all 8 dimensions. Write it down somewhere. A journal, a spreadsheet, a note on your phone, doesn't matter. Just write it down.

```
Week of March 23:
Physical:      7
Psychological: 5
Environmental: 8
Social:        4
Spiritual:     6
Intellectual:  7
Vocational:    5
Financial:     6
TWC:           6.0
```

### Monthly Review

Once a month, look at your weekly scores and ask:
- Which dimension improved?
- Which dimension dropped?
- Is there a pattern? (Do I always dip socially during busy work weeks?)
- What worked that I should keep doing?
- What didn't work that I should change?

### Quarterly Deep Dive

Every three months, do a full assessment. Use the AI-guided self-assessment from [SELF-ASSESSMENT.md](SELF-ASSESSMENT.md) or just sit down with yourself for 30 minutes and really think about each dimension honestly.

Compare your quarterly scores. This is where the real story shows up. Weekly fluctuations are noise. Quarterly trends are signal.

## Common Patterns You'll Notice

**The Seesaw.** When one dimension goes up, another often goes down. You start exercising (Physical up) but have less time for friends (Social down). Awareness of the seesaw lets you plan for it instead of being surprised.

**The Cascade.** One low dimension pulls others down. Financial stress → Psychological anxiety → Social withdrawal → Vocational disengagement. Finding the first domino is key.

**The Mask.** A high dimension hiding a low one. "I'm fine, I just work a lot" might be a Vocational score of 8 masking a Social score of 3. High performance in one area doesn't mean you're thriving overall.

**The Plateau.** Everything sits at 5-6 for weeks. You're surviving but not improving. This usually means you need to make a specific change, not just "try harder." Pick your lowest dimension and apply the healing protocols in [HEALING-PLAYBOOK.md](HEALING-PLAYBOOK.md).

## FAQ

**Do I need to be "into wellness" for this to work?**
No. You need to be honest with yourself about how your life is going across 8 areas. That's it. No incense required.

**What if all my scores are low?**
Start with Physical. Get your sleep, nutrition, and movement to a baseline level. Almost everything else improves when your body is functioning. Then tackle your second-lowest dimension. And don't try to "catch up on sleep" over the weekend. Research shows that after even 5 nights of restricted sleep, inflammatory and stress genes stay dysregulated even after recovery sleep (Cheng et al., SLEEP Oxford, Mar 2026). Consistent sleep beats weekend binges every time.

**What if I don't agree with my AI's scoring?**
Push back. Say "I think my Social should be a 6, not a 4, because..." and explain why. Good AI wellness partners adjust. The score should reflect YOUR reality, not a generic standard.

**How long until I see improvement?**
Most people see noticeable change in their lowest dimension within 2-4 weeks of focused attention. Meaningful, sustained change across multiple dimensions takes 3-6 months. Be patient. You didn't get here overnight.

**Is this therapy?**
No. 8D360 is a wellness framework, not a clinical tool. It works great alongside therapy. If you're dealing with clinical depression, anxiety disorders, PTSD, or other mental health conditions, please work with a licensed professional. Use 8D360 as a complement, not a substitute.

**Can I use this with my therapist?**
Absolutely. Sharing your 8D profile with your therapist gives them a snapshot of your whole life, not just the dimension you happen to bring up in session. Some therapists find this really useful.

## The Most Important Thing

Wellness isn't a destination. It's not a score you reach and then you're done. It's an ongoing process of paying attention, making adjustments, and being honest with yourself.

Your 8D profile will change. Life will change it. A new job, a breakup, a move, a health scare, a spiritual awakening. That's normal. The framework isn't about perfection. It's about awareness.

Know where you are. Know where you're heading. Adjust when something drops. Celebrate when something rises.

That's the whole game.
