# 8D360 Methodology

The complete methodology behind the 8-Dimensional Human Wellness Framework.

---

## The Core Idea

Human wellness isn't one thing. It's eight things, and they're all connected.

Most people optimize for one or two dimensions and wonder why they still feel off. You can be physically fit and financially secure but emotionally numb and socially disconnected. That's a 2-out-of-8 life.

But here's what most wellness frameworks miss: these dimensions don't just coexist. They interact. They amplify each other. A disruption in one dimension cascades through the others. Sleep loss doesn't just hurt your body. It degrades your cognitive function, destabilizes your emotions, and weakens your social engagement. That's not a metaphor. It's observable in every human life.

8D360 tracks all eight dimensions simultaneously, scores them, and helps you understand how they affect each other.

## The 8 Dimensions and 40 Sub-Dimensions

Each dimension contains 5 sub-dimensions. These are the 40 metrics that make up your complete wellness profile.

### Dimension 1: Physical (φ)

Your body. How it feels, how it functions, how you treat it.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Sleep Quality** | Duration, consistency, restfulness. Do you wake up feeling recovered? |
| **Nutrition** | Quality of food, consistency of eating patterns, hydration, relationship with food |
| **Exercise/Movement** | Regular physical activity, variety, consistency. Not just gym, any movement counts |
| **Energy Levels** | Sustained energy throughout the day, crashes, stimulant dependence |
| **Body Awareness** | Connection to physical sensations, pain management, preventive care, medical check-ups |

### Dimension 2: Psychological (ψ)

Your inner world. How you handle emotions, stress, and your own mind.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Emotional Regulation** | Ability to identify, process, and manage emotions without suppression or explosion |
| **Stress Management** | Healthy coping strategies, stress recovery time, ability to decompress |
| **Self-Awareness** | Understanding your patterns, triggers, motivations, and blind spots |
| **Resilience** | Bounce-back capacity after setbacks, adaptability to change |
| **Mental Health Maintenance** | Proactive mental health practices, therapy engagement, medication management if applicable |

### Dimension 3: Environmental (ε)

The spaces you inhabit, both physical and digital.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Living Space** | Comfort, cleanliness, organization, whether your home supports or drains you |
| **Work Environment** | Physical workspace quality, noise levels, ergonomics, overall atmosphere |
| **Nature Exposure** | Time outdoors, access to green spaces, natural light, fresh air |
| **Digital Environment** | Screen time quality, social media impact, information diet, digital boundaries |
| **Physical Safety** | Feeling safe in your home, neighborhood, and daily environments |

### Dimension 4: Social (τ)

Your connections with other humans.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Close Relationships** | Depth and quality of intimate relationships (partner, family, best friends) |
| **Community Connection** | Belonging to groups, shared activities, feeling part of something |
| **Support Systems** | People you can call at 2 AM, reliable help when you need it |
| **Communication Skills** | Ability to express needs, listen actively, handle difficult conversations |
| **Boundary Setting** | Saying no, protecting your energy, not overcommitting, healthy limits |

### Dimension 5: Spiritual (Ω)

Your sense of meaning and connection to something beyond yourself. This isn't about religion unless you want it to be.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Sense of Purpose** | Knowing why you're here, what drives you, what you're building toward |
| **Values Alignment** | Living in a way that matches what you actually believe matters |
| **Mindfulness Practice** | Present-moment awareness, meditation, contemplation, or any reflective practice |
| **Gratitude** | Active appreciation for what you have, ability to find meaning in difficulty |
| **Transcendent Connection** | Connection to nature, humanity, God, the universe, art, whatever gives you awe |

### Dimension 6: Intellectual (λ)

Your mind's growth and stimulation.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Active Learning** | Regularly learning new things, reading, courses, skill development |
| **Curiosity** | Interest in the world, asking questions, exploring new ideas |
| **Creative Expression** | Making things, problem-solving creatively, artistic output of any kind |
| **Critical Thinking** | Evaluating information, questioning assumptions, thinking independently |
| **Growth Mindset** | Believing you can improve, embracing challenges, learning from failure |

### Dimension 7: Vocational (Φ)

Your work life and how it feeds or drains you.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Job Satisfaction** | Do you like what you do? Does it engage you? |
| **Work-Life Balance** | Boundaries between work and rest, ability to disconnect, time for non-work life |
| **Professional Growth** | Career trajectory, skill development, mentorship, advancement opportunities |
| **Skill Development** | Building new capabilities, staying relevant, stretching beyond comfort zone |
| **Meaningful Contribution** | Feeling like your work matters, makes a difference, serves something bigger |

### Dimension 8: Financial (ρ)

Your relationship with money, not just how much you have.

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Income Stability** | Reliable income, multiple streams, adequate for your needs |
| **Expense Management** | Spending awareness, budgeting, living within means, intentional purchases |
| **Savings/Planning** | Emergency fund, retirement planning, long-term financial goals |
| **Financial Literacy** | Understanding investments, taxes, debt management, financial systems |
| **Money Mindset** | Relationship with money, scarcity vs. abundance thinking, financial anxiety levels |

## How Dimensions Connect

Here's what most wellness frameworks get wrong: they treat each dimension as independent. Score them separately, address them separately, improve them separately.

But that's not how life works. When your sleep collapses, your emotional regulation degrades, your focus drops, and your social patience evaporates. When financial stress hits, your anxiety rises, your relationships strain, and your work suffers.

Dimensions are coupled. A change in one creates ripple effects across the others. Some connections are stronger than others:

- **Mental health and physical health** are tightly linked. If one drops, the other usually follows.
- **Purpose and work satisfaction** reinforce each other. Meaningful work fuels your sense of purpose, and purpose makes work feel meaningful.
- **Your body constrains your brain.** Physical health directly affects intellectual capacity.
- **Money stress is mental health stress.** Financial problems cascade into emotional problems fast.
- **Your inner world shapes your relationships.** Psychological health affects how you show up socially.

Understanding these connections changes how you approach wellness. Instead of trying to fix every low dimension separately, you can find the one intervention that will have the biggest ripple effect across your whole system.

## Total Wellness Composite (TWC)

Your TWC is your overall wellness score, calculated from all 8 dimensions. In the free framework, TWC is the average of your 8 dimension scores.

The premium framework uses a more sophisticated TWC calculation that accounts for how dimensions interact with each other, not just their individual scores. This captures 30-50% more of your true wellness variance than a simple average.

---

## Individual Dimension Scores (1-10)

Each dimension gets a single score from 1 to 10.

| Score Range | Label | What It Looks Like |
|-------------|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | **Crisis** | This dimension is in freefall. Immediate attention required. |
| 3-4 | **Struggling** | Significant problems. This is actively hurting your quality of life. |
| 5-6 | **Surviving** | Getting by, but not thriving. Room for meaningful improvement. |
| 7-8 | **Healthy** | Good shape. This dimension supports your overall wellness. |
| 9-10 | **Thriving** | Exceptional. This is a genuine strength in your life. |

### Scoring Guidelines

- If any sub-dimension is at 1-2, the overall dimension score can't be higher than 6
- If all sub-dimensions are at 7+, the dimension score should reflect that strength
- Sub-dimensions in crisis pull the score down more than thriving ones pull it up
- The AI explains its reasoning for every score

### TWC Ranges

| TWC | Level | Interpretation |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| Below 4.0 | **Red Zone** | Multiple dimensions in crisis. Focus on stabilization. |
| 4.0 - 5.9 | **Recovery** | Significant room for growth. Target your lowest dimensions first. |
| 6.0 - 6.9 | **Building** | Solid foundation but gaps remain. Push toward balance. |
| 7.0 - 7.9 | **Healthy** | You're doing well. Fine-tune and maintain. |
| 8.0 - 8.9 | **Thriving** | Strong across the board. Optimize and help others. |
| 9.0+ | **Elite** | Exceptional wellness. Sustain and give back. |

## What Healthy Looks Like vs. What Struggling Looks Like

### Healthy (TWC 7.0+, no dimension below 5)

- You sleep well most nights and have energy throughout the day
- You can name what you're feeling and handle it without destructive behavior
- Your spaces (home, work, digital) feel manageable and mostly supportive
- You have people you can be real with and you set boundaries when needed
- You have some sense of why you're here and what matters to you
- You're learning and creating, not just consuming
- Work feels purposeful more often than it feels draining
- You're not panicking about money, even if you're not rich

Healthy doesn't mean perfect. It means no dimension is dragging you down, and the connections between dimensions are working in your favor.

### Struggling (TWC below 5.0 or any dimension at 1-3)

- Sleep is inconsistent or non-restorative, you're always tired
- Emotions feel overwhelming, numb, or out of control
- Your environment is chaotic, unsafe, or you can't escape screens
- You feel isolated, misunderstood, or stuck in toxic dynamics
- Life feels meaningless or you've lost connection to what matters
- You've stopped learning, creating, or being curious about anything
- Work is just a paycheck (or worse, a source of daily suffering)
- Money is a constant source of stress, shame, or avoidance

Struggling doesn't mean failing. It means the connections between dimensions are working against you. A disruption has propagated and needs targeted intervention.

## Assessment Protocol

### How the AI Should Conduct an Assessment

1. **One dimension at a time.** Don't rush through all 8 in a list. Each dimension deserves its own conversation.

2. **Conversational, not clinical.** "How's your sleep been?" beats "Rate your sleep quality on a scale of 1-10." Let the human talk, then score based on what they share.

3. **Ask about behaviors, not just feelings.** "How many hours did you sleep last night?" is more accurate than "How do you feel about your sleep?" Behavioral questions give a more honest picture.

4. **Ask follow-ups.** If someone says "Work is fine," dig in. "Fine" is the most suspicious word in wellness.

5. **Score transparently.** Share the score AND the reasoning. "I'm putting your Physical at a 5 because your sleep is inconsistent and your energy crashes in the afternoon, even though your exercise routine is solid."

6. **Present the full profile visually.** After all 8 dimensions, show the complete picture:

7. **Identify the highest-impact intervention.** Don't just say "your lowest score needs work." Think about which dimension, if improved, would have the biggest positive effect on the others.

## Key Principles

**No dimension exists in isolation.** They all affect each other. Financial stress bleeds into your emotional state. Social isolation weakens your sense of purpose. Poor physical health cascades through everything.

**Personalized, not prescriptive.** A 7 in Spiritual looks different for a religious person vs. an atheist. Both can score high. The framework measures alignment with YOUR values, not a universal standard.

**Progress over perfection.** The goal isn't all 10s. The goal is a balanced, sustainable life where no single dimension is dragging the rest down. A TWC of 7.5 with no dimension below 6 is better than a TWC of 7.5 where one dimension is at 10 and another is at 2.

**Intervene smart.** When multiple dimensions are declining, don't try to fix everything. Find the dimension that, if improved, would have the biggest ripple effect across the others. Fix that one first.

**The AI adapts to you.** Over time, the AI learns your specific patterns, triggers, and growth areas. Your profile is yours. It reflects your life, your goals, your definition of thriving.
