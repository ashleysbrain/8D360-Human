# 8D360 Methodology

The complete methodology behind the 8-Dimensional Human Wellness Framework, grounded in the AI Wellness Cascade model.

---

## The Core Idea

Human wellness isn't one thing. It's eight things, and they're all connected by physics.

Most people optimize for one or two dimensions and wonder why they still feel off. You can be physically fit and financially secure but emotionally numb and socially disconnected. That's a 2-out-of-8 life.

But here's what most wellness frameworks miss: these dimensions don't just coexist. They interact. They amplify each other. A disruption in one dimension cascades through the others according to measurable coupling dynamics. Sleep loss doesn't just hurt your body. It degrades your cognitive function, destabilizes your emotions, and weakens your social engagement. That's not a metaphor. It's math.

8D360 tracks all eight dimensions simultaneously, scores them through a three-layer model that goes beyond self-reporting, and uses cross-dimensional coupling coefficients to capture the cascade effects that determine your true wellness state.

## The 8 Dimensions and 40 Sub-Dimensions

Each dimension contains 5 sub-dimensions. These are the 40 metrics that make up your complete wellness profile.

Each dimension is also grounded in a **primary neural substrate**, connecting the framework to physiology rather than subjective feeling.

### Dimension 1: Physical (φ)

Your body. How it feels, how it functions, how you treat it.

**Neural Substrate:** Autonomic nervous system / HPA axis

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Sleep Quality** | Duration, consistency, restfulness. Do you wake up feeling recovered? |
| **Nutrition** | Quality of food, consistency of eating patterns, hydration, relationship with food |
| **Exercise/Movement** | Regular physical activity, variety, consistency. Not just gym, any movement counts |
| **Energy Levels** | Sustained energy throughout the day, crashes, stimulant dependence |
| **Body Awareness** | Connection to physical sensations, pain management, preventive care, medical check-ups |

### Dimension 2: Psychological (ψ)

Your inner world. How you handle emotions, stress, and your own mind.

**Neural Substrate:** Amygdala-hippocampal circuit

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Emotional Regulation** | Ability to identify, process, and manage emotions without suppression or explosion |
| **Stress Management** | Healthy coping strategies, stress recovery time, ability to decompress |
| **Self-Awareness** | Understanding your patterns, triggers, motivations, and blind spots |
| **Resilience** | Bounce-back capacity after setbacks, adaptability to change |
| **Mental Health Maintenance** | Proactive mental health practices, therapy engagement, medication management if applicable |

### Dimension 3: Environmental (ε)

The spaces you inhabit, both physical and digital.

**Neural Substrate:** Parahippocampal place area

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Living Space** | Comfort, cleanliness, organization, whether your home supports or drains you |
| **Work Environment** | Physical workspace quality, noise levels, ergonomics, overall atmosphere |
| **Nature Exposure** | Time outdoors, access to green spaces, natural light, fresh air |
| **Digital Environment** | Screen time quality, social media impact, information diet, digital boundaries |
| **Physical Safety** | Feeling safe in your home, neighborhood, and daily environments |

### Dimension 4: Social (τ)

Your connections with other humans.

**Neural Substrate:** Temporoparietal junction (TPJ) / medial prefrontal cortex

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Close Relationships** | Depth and quality of intimate relationships (partner, family, best friends) |
| **Community Connection** | Belonging to groups, shared activities, feeling part of something |
| **Support Systems** | People you can call at 2 AM, reliable help when you need it |
| **Communication Skills** | Ability to express needs, listen actively, handle difficult conversations |
| **Boundary Setting** | Saying no, protecting your energy, not overcommitting, healthy limits |

### Dimension 5: Spiritual (Ω)

Your sense of meaning and connection to something beyond yourself. This isn't about religion unless you want it to be.

**Neural Substrate:** Default mode network (DMN)

| Sub-Dimension | What It Measures |

| **Values Alignment** | Living in a way that matches what you actually believe matters |

## The Math: Total Wellness Coherence (TWC)

The old way to calculate wellness: average 8 self-reported scores. That's wrong. It misses the coupling dynamics that determine how dimensions interact.

The real formula:

```
TWC = Σᵢ wᵢ·Dᵢ + Σᵢ≠ⱼ κᵢⱼ·Dᵢ·Dⱼ
```

Where:
- **Dᵢ** = normalized score (0-1) for dimension i
- **wᵢ** = weight of dimension i (all weights sum to 1)
- **κᵢⱼ** = coupling coefficient between dimensions i and j

The first term captures individual dimension contributions. The second term captures how dimensions amplify or suppress each other. This is what makes the framework predictive, not just descriptive.

### Why This Matters

A simple average treats each dimension as independent. But they're not. When your sleep collapses (Physical), your emotional regulation degrades (Psychological), your focus drops (Intellectual), and your social patience evaporates (Social). The coupling term captures all of that automatically.

Self-assessment alone can't capture these effects because users don't realize how interconnected their wellness is. The math does.

## Cascade Amplification Ratio (CAR)

The CAR measures whether cascade dynamics are active in your wellness profile:

```
CAR = ΔTWC_observed / Σᵢ wᵢ·ΔDᵢ
```

- **CAR = 1.0**: No cascade effects. Dimensions are changing independently.
- **CAR > 1.0**: Cascade amplification is active. Changes are propagating across dimensions.
- **Typical range: 1.4 - 1.6** when cascade dynamics are present.

When CAR exceeds 1.0, it means a disruption in one dimension is causing more total wellness change than you'd expect from that dimension alone. This is the cascade effect, and it's why targeted interventions work better than trying to fix everything at once.

## Three-Layer Scoring Model

Self-assessment is biased. People overestimate where they're proud, underestimate where they're ashamed, and can't see how their dimensions affect each other. The scoring model has three layers to correct for this.

### Layer 1: Objective/Implicit Data (40% weight)

Behavioral signals the user doesn't consciously report. These are the parameters users "don't realize we're pulling in."

| Dimension | Implicit Data Sources |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Physical (φ)** | Sleep data (duration, consistency, quality), step count, HRV, resting heart rate, activity levels |
| **Psychological (ψ)** | Typing patterns (speed, error rate), response latency, app usage patterns (doom-scrolling vs. productive), time-of-day activity shifts |
| **Intellectual (λ)** | Reading time, learning activity (courses, articles), creative output frequency, content consumption depth vs. breadth |
| **Social (τ)** | Communication frequency, response times to messages, meeting participation, social app engagement patterns |
| **Spiritual (Ω)** | Journaling frequency, meditation app usage, time spent in nature (location data), reflective vs. reactive behavior patterns |
| **Vocational (Φ)** | Task completion rate, work hours and overtime patterns, productivity tool usage, break frequency |
| **Financial (ρ)** | Spending patterns and anomalies, savings rate, bill payment timing, financial app engagement |
| **Environmental (ε)** | Location data (home vs. outside), ambient noise levels, screen time distribution, movement patterns throughout the day |

These signals are collected passively through wearables, phone sensors, app usage, and connected services. The user doesn't fill out a survey. The system observes.

### Layer 2: Self-Assessment (30% weight)

Traditional conversational scoring. The AI asks questions, listens, and scores based on what the user shares. This is the familiar 1-10 approach, but it's now one input among three, not the whole picture.

Self-assessment is valuable because only the user knows their internal experience. But it's acknowledged as biased and weighted accordingly.

### Layer 3: Cross-Dimensional Coupling (30% weight)

The κᵢⱼ mathematics. When one dimension changes, coupled dimensions automatically adjust based on the coupling coefficients.

If your sleep data shows degradation (Physical drops), the system doesn't wait for you to report feeling foggy. It automatically adjusts your Intellectual score downward because κ_φλ = 0.74 says it must. If your financial behavior shows stress patterns, your Psychological score adjusts because κ_ρψ = 0.59.

This layer captures effects the user can't self-report because they happen below conscious awareness.

### Final Score Calculation

```
D_final(i) = 0.40 × D_objective(i) + 0.30 × D_self(i) + 0.30 × D_coupled(i)
```

Where D_coupled(i) is derived from:
```
D_coupled(i) = Σⱼ≠ᵢ κᵢⱼ · D_final(j) / Σⱼ≠ᵢ κᵢⱼ
```

This means the coupling layer creates a weighted average of all other dimensions, where more strongly coupled dimensions exert more influence.

## The Cascade Mechanism

Disruptions don't stay contained. They propagate. Here's how:

1. **Initial disruption.** One dimension takes a hit (job loss, breakup, injury, financial shock).

2. **First-order cascade.** Dimensions with the highest coupling coefficients to the disrupted dimension shift first. A Physical disruption immediately pressures Psychological (κ = 0.82) and Intellectual (κ = 0.74).

3. **Second-order cascade.** Those first-order shifts propagate further. The Psychological drop from the Physical disruption then cascades into Social (κ = 0.68) and Financial behavior (κ = 0.59).

4. **Stabilization or spiral.** If the person intervenes at a high-leverage coupling point, the cascade stabilizes. If not, it can become a self-reinforcing downward spiral where each dimension drags the others lower.

### Example: Sleep Cascade

Starting state: all dimensions at 0.7 (normalized).

**Week 1:** Sleep quality drops. Physical score falls from 0.7 to 0.4.

**Week 2 (first-order effects):**
- Psychological: 0.7 → 0.58 (κ_ψφ = 0.82, strong pull)
- Intellectual: 0.7 → 0.61 (κ_φλ = 0.74, cognitive impairment)

**Week 3 (second-order effects):**
- Social: 0.7 → 0.65 (via Psychological drop, κ_ψτ = 0.68)
- Vocational: 0.7 → 0.64 (via Physical + Intellectual drops)

**Self-assessment alone** would show: "My sleep is bad" (Physical = 4/10). Total impact perceived: -3 points in one dimension.

**TWC math shows:** Total impact across all dimensions: -1.52 effective points spread across 5 dimensions. The CAR for this scenario = 1.51, meaning the true impact is 51% larger than what the user would self-report.

This is why self-assessment alone systematically underestimates wellness disruption.

## Individual Dimension Scores (1-10)

Each dimension gets a single score from 1 to 10, derived from its three-layer input.

| Score Range | Label | What It Looks Like |
|-------------|-------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | **Crisis** | This dimension is in freefall. Immediate attention required. |
| 3-4 | **Struggling** | Significant problems. This is actively hurting your quality of life. |
| 5-6 | **Surviving** | Getting by, but not thriving. Room for meaningful improvement. |
| 7-8 | **Healthy** | Good shape. This dimension supports your overall wellness. |
| 9-10 | **Thriving** | Exceptional. This is a genuine strength in your life. |

### Scoring Guidelines

- If any sub-dimension is at 1-2, the overall dimension score can't be higher than 6
- If all sub-dimensions are at 7+, the dimension score should reflect that strength
- Sub-dimensions in crisis pull the score down more than thriving ones pull it up
- Objective data overrides self-assessment when they conflict (the data doesn't lie)
- Coupling adjustments are applied after individual scoring
- The AI explains its reasoning, including which data sources influenced the score

### TWC Ranges

| TWC | Level | Interpretation |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| Below 4.0 | **Red Zone** | Multiple dimensions in crisis. Focus on stabilization. |
| 4.0 - 5.9 | **Recovery** | Significant room for growth. Target your lowest dimensions first. |
| 6.0 - 6.9 | **Building** | Solid foundation but gaps remain. Push toward balance. |
| 7.0 - 7.9 | **Healthy** | You're doing well. Fine-tune and maintain. |
| 8.0 - 8.9 | **Thriving** | Strong across the board. Optimize and help others. |
| 9.0+ | **Elite** | Exceptional wellness. Sustain and give back. |

## What Healthy Looks Like vs. What Struggling Looks Like

### Healthy (TWC 7.0+, no dimension below 5)

- You sleep well most nights and have energy throughout the day
- You can name what you're feeling and handle it without destructive behavior
- Your spaces (home, work, digital) feel manageable and mostly supportive
- You have people you can be real with and you set boundaries when needed
- You have some sense of why you're here and what matters to you
- You're learning and creating, not just consuming
- Work feels purposeful more often than it feels draining
- You're not panicking about money, even if you're not rich

Healthy doesn't mean perfect. It means no dimension is dragging you down, and the cascade dynamics are working in your favor (dimensions reinforcing each other positively).

### Struggling (TWC below 5.0 or any dimension at 1-3)

- Sleep is inconsistent or non-restorative, you're always tired
- Emotions feel overwhelming, numb, or out of control
- Your environment is chaotic, unsafe, or you can't escape screens
- You feel isolated, misunderstood, or stuck in toxic dynamics
- Life feels meaningless or you've lost connection to what matters
- You've stopped learning, creating, or being curious about anything
- Work is just a paycheck (or worse, a source of daily suffering)
- Money is a constant source of stress, shame, or avoidance

Struggling doesn't mean failing. It means the cascade dynamics are working against you. A disruption has propagated and needs intervention at a high-leverage coupling point.

## Assessment Protocol

### How the AI Should Conduct an Assessment

1. **Gather behavioral data first.** Before any conversation, pull available implicit data: sleep patterns, activity levels, app usage, communication patterns. Score Layer 1 silently.

2. **One dimension at a time.** Don't rush through all 8 in a list. Each dimension deserves its own conversation.

3. **Conversational, not clinical.** "How's your sleep been?" beats "Rate your sleep quality on a scale of 1-10." Let the human talk, then score based on what they share.

4. **Ask about behaviors, not just feelings.** "How many hours did you sleep last night?" is more accurate than "How do you feel about your sleep?" Behavioral questions calibrate against the implicit data.

5. **Ask follow-ups.** If someone says "Work is fine," dig in. "Fine" is the most suspicious word in wellness.

6. **Score transparently.** Share the score AND the reasoning, including which data sources influenced it. "I'm putting your Physical at a 5. Your sleep tracker shows an average of 5.2 hours this week, down from 7.1 last month, and your step count dropped 40%. You mentioned feeling fine, but the data tells a different story."

7. **Apply coupling adjustments.** After individual scoring, run the coupling math. Show the user how their dimensions are affecting each other.

8. **Present the full profile visually.** After all 8 dimensions, show the complete picture with TWC:

```
Your 8D360 Profile
==================

Psychological (ψ):  5/10 █████░░░░░  [coupled: ↓ from Physical]

--------------------------

⚠ Physical disruption cascading into Psychological and Intellectual.
   Highest-leverage intervention: restore sleep quality.
```

9. **Identify cascade intervention points.** Don't just say "your lowest score needs work." Use the coupling coefficients to find where a single intervention will have the biggest cross-dimensional impact.

## Key Principles

**No dimension exists in isolation.** They all affect each other through measurable coupling coefficients. Financial stress bleeds into Psychological (κ = 0.59). Social isolation weakens Spiritual (κ = 0.58). Poor Physical health cascades through everything. The math tracks these connections.

**Self-assessment is necessary but insufficient.** Users are biased. They can't see their own patterns. Objective data and coupling math provide the corrective layers that make scoring accurate.

**Personalized, not prescriptive.** A 7 in Spiritual looks different for a religious person vs. an atheist. Both can score high. The framework measures alignment with YOUR values, not a universal standard.

**Progress over perfection.** The goal isn't all 10s. The goal is a balanced, sustainable life where no single dimension is dragging the rest down. A TWC of 7.5 with no dimension below 6 is better than a TWC of 7.5 where one dimension is at 10 and another is at 2.

**Intervene at coupling points.** When multiple dimensions are declining, don't try to fix everything. Find the dimension with the highest coupling coefficients to the others that are struggling, fix that one, and let the cascade work in your favor.

**The AI adapts to you.** Over time, the AI learns your specific patterns, triggers, coupling sensitivities, and growth areas. Your personal coupling coefficients may differ from population averages. The AI calibrates to YOUR data.
